Plain-language policies

Clear expectations make better projects.

Last updated June 29, 2026. Iluminity currently operates as an independent web design service, not as a corporation or law firm.

1. Project agreement controls

Website descriptions and prices on this site are general information, not a binding offer. Work begins only after both parties accept a project-specific written proposal or service agreement stating deliverables, price, payment schedule, revision limits and estimated timeline. If these website terms conflict with a signed project agreement, the signed agreement controls.

2. Scope and changes

Only deliverables expressly listed in the accepted scope are included. New pages, integrations, features, redesigns or requests outside that scope may require a written change order, additional payment and a revised schedule. Silence, informal discussion or a demo feature does not add work to the scope.

3. Client responsibilities

Clients are responsible for timely delivery and accuracy of approved names, claims, prices, legal notices, images, credentials and other content. The client represents that it has permission to use supplied materials. Delays in feedback, access or content may move delivery dates.

4. Payments, scheduling and cancellation

Deposits reserve production time and are generally non-refundable once work or scheduling begins, except where applicable law requires otherwise. Invoices are due according to the project agreement. Work may pause for overdue payments. Final files, credentials, transfer and launch may be withheld until all amounts due are paid.

5. Revisions and acceptance

Revision rounds are limited to the number stated in the project agreement and must be submitted as consolidated feedback. A project is considered accepted when the client approves it in writing, launches it, uses the deliverables publicly, or does not report an agreed-scope defect within the stated review period.

6. Results and third parties

No specific sales, revenue, lead volume, search ranking, platform approval or business outcome is guaranteed. Third-party services—including hosts, domains, email providers, payment processors, plugins and social platforms—are governed by their own terms, availability and fees. Iluminity is not responsible for third-party outages, policy changes or account actions.

7. Intellectual property

After full payment, the client receives the rights expressly granted in the project agreement to final custom deliverables. Iluminity retains ownership of pre-existing tools, reusable code, systems, know-how, unused concepts and general design methods. Unless the agreement says otherwise, Iluminity may display completed public work in its portfolio.

8. Warranty and correction period

Iluminity will correct reproducible defects that materially prevent agreed functionality during the correction period stated in the project agreement, typically 14 days after launch. This does not cover new requests, client edits, third-party changes, unsupported devices or content errors approved by the client.

9. Limitation of liability

To the maximum extent permitted by applicable law, Iluminity will not be liable for indirect, incidental, special or consequential damages, lost profits, lost data or business interruption. Iluminity’s aggregate liability relating to a project will not exceed the amount the client actually paid for that project. Some jurisdictions do not permit certain limitations, so those limitations apply only to the extent legally allowed.

10. Disputes and governing terms

The parties should first attempt to resolve concerns through good-faith written discussion. Any governing law, venue, mediation or arbitration terms should be stated in the project-specific agreement based on the parties’ locations. Nothing on this page removes rights that cannot legally be waived.
