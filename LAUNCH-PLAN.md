# Iluminity launch plan

## Positioning

Iluminity is presented publicly as an **independent web design studio** focused on US local-service businesses. Do not use “Inc.”, “LLC”, “corporation”, “certified agency” or other legal/status language unless that status is formally obtained.

## Initial sales funnel

1. Prospect lands on the homepage.
2. Opens the industry library.
3. Compares three complete demos.
4. Clicks “Request this design”.
5. Gmail opens with industry and model already identified.
6. Iluminity replies with three questions, a recommended package and a call option.
7. Client receives a written proposal/agreement.
8. Deposit is collected manually through an agreed provider.
9. Work begins after payment and content receipt.

## Recommended initial offer

- Lead with the $1,250 Business package.
- Keep the $650 Landing package as a low-friction entry.
- Use the $2,400 Growth package to anchor value and serve larger clients.
- Never promise rankings, leads or revenue.
- Sell clarity, speed, industry fit and direct communication.

## Google Sheets role

Use Sheets as a lightweight content manager and internal catalog—not as a high-security customer database.

- Public catalog data: acceptable.
- Leads with basic business information: acceptable with careful access control.
- Passwords, payment data, identity documents or sensitive client credentials: never store them in the Sheet.
- Keep a JSON backup before major catalog changes.

## Before public outreach

- Replace placeholder demo reviews with clearly labeled demonstrations or real authorized testimonials.
- Verify every external image license or replace it with client-owned/licensed imagery.
- Have the service agreement reviewed for the freelancer’s real jurisdiction.
- Create a dedicated professional email signature.
- Set up a simple spreadsheet for lead status: New, Replied, Call, Proposal, Won, Lost.
- Test every Gmail and Instagram link on mobile.

## When revenue begins

- Purchase the domain.
- Register the appropriate local business form when justified.
- Open a separate business payment account.
- Move commercial hosting to a plan whose terms permit business use.
- Add professional liability coverage if appropriate for project size and jurisdiction.
